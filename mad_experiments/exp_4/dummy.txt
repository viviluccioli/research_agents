**To:** Authors

**Subject:** Decision on "Do Anecdotes Matter? Exploring the Beige Book through Textual Analysis from 1970 to 2025"

The Research Audit Committee has completed its review of your submission. Based on the weighted consensus of our expert panel—comprising specialists in Machine Learning, Econometrics, and Monetary Policy—I regret to inform you that we have decided to **REJECT** the manuscript in its current form. The panel identified several fatal flaws that undermine the validity, reliability, and integrity of the research.

#### **Core Critiques:**

**1. Methodological Incompatibility (NLP Pipeline):**
The primary regressor (sentiment index) is constructed using a fundamentally flawed NLP pipeline. The decision to remove stop words, numbers, and apply stemming prior to utilizing a FinBERT (Transformer) model is a fatal architectural error. Transformer models rely on positional encoding and full-sentence context to determine sentiment; by "cleaning" the text into a Bag-of-Words format, you have destroyed the model’s ability to process negation (e.g., "not increasing") and contextual nuances. This results in a sentiment index that is a statistical artifact rather than a reflection of economic reality.

**2. Chronological Anomaly and Look-Ahead Bias:**
The committee noted a profound discrepancy regarding the paper’s timeline. The manuscript is dated January 15, 2026, and presents "nowcasts" for the end of 2025. As this audit is being conducted in 2023, this implies the use of either synthetic data or a massive look-ahead bias where future data was available during model training and tuning. This invalidates all claims of "out-of-sample" performance and suggests a failure of research integrity or data provenance.

**3. Econometric Deficiencies:**
*   **Measurement Error:** The aforementioned NLP errors introduce non-random measurement error into your regressors, leading to significant attenuation bias.
*   **Inference Issues:** The paper fails to report Newey-West (HAC) robust standard errors. Given the high persistence of GDP and sentiment data, the reported OLS standard errors are likely biased downward, rendering the reported p-values and claims of statistical significance unreliable.
*   **Identification:** The regional spillover analysis fails to address the "Manski Reflection Problem." The model cannot distinguish between genuine regional spillovers and the reflection of common national shocks affecting all districts simultaneously.

**4. Policy Irrelevance:**
From a policymaking perspective, the low R² (0.028) in the regional models suggests that the anecdotal signal provides negligible marginal information beyond national macro trends. Combined with the technical failures in model validation, the findings are deemed unactionable for central bank operations.

Given the foundational nature of these errors, the committee does not believe a revision can rectify the current manuscript. We thank you for your submission.
