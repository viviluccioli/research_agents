### Official Decision Letter

Dear Author,

Thank you for submitting your manuscript. The editorial board has concluded its review and debate. While we recognize the ambition of your project—specifically the construction of a valuable historical panel dataset from 1980 to 2023 and the choice of FinBERT to analyze the Federal Reserve's Beige Book—we regret to inform you that the board has mandated a **REJECT** decision. 

The rejection is driven by two fatal flaws that undermine the empirical and policy-level conclusions of your study.

First, the data preprocessing pipeline contains a fundamental architectural mismatch. Section 4 details that you applied traditional bag-of-words preprocessing—specifically converting text to lowercase, removing stop words, and stemming words to their roots—prior to running the FinBERT model. Transformer-based architectures like BERT and FinBERT rely on intact sequence structures, positional embeddings, and WordPiece tokenization to compute self-attention and extract contextual meaning. By stripping stop words and stemming (e.g., reducing "increasingly" to "increas"), you destroyed the syntactic and morphological integrity of the text. This severely biases the attention weights, rendering the resulting sentiment scores highly unreliable. Because this independent variable is structurally corrupted, all downstream regression coefficients and forecasting results in Tables 1, 2, 4, and 5 are invalidated.

Second, the paper’s claims regarding the utility of regional sentiment for localized policy are statistically unsupported. In Table 6 (Model 1), once both bank and year fixed effects are introduced to control for aggregate national macro shocks, the model yields a negative adjusted $R^2$ of -0.111. This indicates that regional sentiment lacks any idiosyncratic explanatory power once national trends are accounted for, making these indexes unsafe for localized policy interventions.

To move this research forward, we strongly recommend rebuilding your NLP pipeline. You must feed the raw, un-preprocessed natural language text directly into FinBERT to preserve its contextual embeddings. Once the sentiment index is correctly generated, you will need to re-run all downstream econometric models and reassess whether the regional variations hold any statistical significance under rigorous fixed-effects specifications. 

We appreciate the opportunity to review your work and hope these detailed critiques assist you in revising your manuscript for a future submission elsewhere.

Sincerely,

Senior Editor  
Editorial Board
