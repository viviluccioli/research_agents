## Section: Data ⭐
| Criterion | Weight | Importance Multiplier | Description |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|-------------|
| Source | 15% | **1.2×** | Data source clearly identified and described |
| Appropriateness | 25% | **1.2×** | Data suitable for the research question with clear justification (5=ideal data with detailed justification, 3=adequate but with limitations, 1=questionable fit) |
| Limitations | 30% | **1.2×** | Selection bias, measurement error, coverage gaps honestly acknowledged with depth (5=thorough discussion of implications, 3=mentioned but superficial, 1=ignored or dismissed) |

| Robustness Depth | 20% | **1.3×** | Robustness approach is comprehensive and pre-specified (5=extensive sensitivity analysis across specifications/samples/measures, 3=basic checks, 1=minimal or ad hoc) |
| Replicability | 10% | **1.3×** | Sufficient detail for replication |

**Effective Weight:** CRITICAL section (1.3×) — highest importance for empirical papers

**NEW CRITERIA:**
- `Robustness Depth` (was "Robustness Plan") - Now emphasizes comprehensiveness and depth
- All criteria now have 5/3/1 scoring anchors for discriminating evaluation

| Criterion | Weight | Importance Multiplier | Description |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|-------------|
| Alignment | 20% | **1.2×** | Results directly address the stated research question |
| Interpretation Depth | 30% | **1.2×** | Results interpreted with depth: economic magnitude quantified, mechanisms explained, compared to prior work (5=multi-faceted interpretation with decomposition, 3=basic magnitude discussion, 1=only reports significance stars) |

- `Honesty` (replaces "Anomalies") - Emphasizes transparency about null/contradictory findings
- `Calibration` - NEW criterion for real-world context

## Section: Proofs / Derivations ⭐⭐⭐ (HIGHEST FOR THEORETICAL)
| Criterion | Weight | Importance Multiplier | Description |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|-------------|
| Correctness | 20% | **1.4×** | No mathematical errors in derivations |
| Rigor | 25% | **1.4×** | Technical conditions handled properly: convergence explicitly derived, transversality stated, edge cases addressed (5=complete rigorous treatment, 3=basic correct derivation, 1=hand-waving) |
| Economic Depth | 25% | **1.4×** | Economic mechanisms explained with depth beyond surface intuition: provides decomposition, welfare analysis, multi-layered interpretation (5=profound insights, 3=adequate one-sentence explanation, 1=tautological) |
| Sophistication | 15% | **1.4×** | Analysis advances beyond routine techniques (5=novel theoretical insight, 3=competent standard analysis, 1=trivial) |
| Clarity | 15% | **1.4×** | Derivation clear and accessible to expert readers |

**Effective Weight:** MOST CRITICAL section (1.4×) — highest importance for theoretical papers

**MAJOR CHANGES:**
- **Correctness** weight reduced from 35% to 20% (correctness alone doesn't indicate quality)
- **NEW: Rigor** (25%) - Measures depth of technical treatment (convergence, transversality, edge cases)
- **NEW: Economic Depth** (25%) - Measures quality of economic interpretation (decomposition, welfare, multi-layered)
- **NEW: Sophistication** (15%) - Measures theoretical advancement beyond routine
- **Clarity** weight reduced from 25% to 15%
- **Removed: Logical Flow (was 25%), Completeness (was 20%), Intuition (was 20%)**
  - These are now integrated into Rigor, Economic Depth, and Clarity

# KEY IMPROVEMENTS (March 2024)

## 1. New Depth-Focused Criteria

### Theoretical Papers (Proofs section)
- **Rigor** (25%): Measures technical completeness (convergence, transversality, edge cases)
- **Economic Depth** (25%): Measures quality of economic interpretation (decomposition, welfare, multi-layered)
- **Sophistication** (15%): Measures theoretical advancement beyond routine

### Empirical Papers
- **Interpretation Depth** (30% in Results): Emphasizes multi-faceted analysis
- **Robustness Depth** (20% in Methodology): Emphasizes comprehensiveness
- **Calibration** (15% in Results): NEW criterion for real-world context

## 2. Concrete Scoring Anchors (5/3/1)

All major criteria now include explicit examples:
- **5 (Excellent)**: Publication-ready, rigorous, insightful
- **3 (Adequate)**: Meets minimum standards, technically correct but routine
- **1 (Poor)**: Fundamental flaws

## 3. Expected Impact on Scores

### High-Quality Papers (e.g., Rithika's prompts)
- **Before**: ~3.5 average (everything technically correct → 3-4)
- **After**: ~4.5 average (depth rewarded: convergence conditions, decomposition, calibration)

### Low-Quality Papers (e.g., Andrew's prompts)
- **Before**: ~3.0 average (technically correct → 3)
- **After**: ~2.5 average (lack of depth penalized: ad hoc parameters, superficial intuition)

### Score Spread
- **Before**: 0.5 points (compressed)
- **After**: 2.0 points (discriminating)
