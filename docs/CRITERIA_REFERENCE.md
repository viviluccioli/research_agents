# EMPIRICAL PAPER CRITERIA

## Data
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Source** | 15% | Data source clearly identified and described |
| **Appropriateness** | 25% | Data suitable for the research question with clear justification |
| **Limitations** | 30% | Selection bias, measurement error, coverage gaps honestly acknowledged with depth |
| **Sample** | 15% | Sample size, time period, geographic scope clear |
| **Variables** | 15% | Key variables operationally defined with measurement details |

**Section Importance Multiplier:** 1.2

**Scoring Anchors:**
- **Appropriateness (5)**: Ideal or near-ideal data with detailed justification of fit; thorough discussion of why this data answers the question
- **Appropriateness (3)**: Adequate data with basic justification; limitations mentioned
- **Appropriateness (1)**: Questionable fit with limitations ignored or dismissed
- **Limitations (5)**: Thorough discussion of implications for inference; selection processes transparent; measurement issues addressed
- **Limitations (3)**: Mentioned but discussion somewhat superficial
- **Limitations (1)**: Ignored or dismissed; inadequate source description

**Common Issues to Look For:**
- Missing data source documentation
- No discussion of sample selection criteria
- Undefined or poorly operationalized variables
- Inadequate treatment of measurement error
- No discussion of data limitations

## Methodology
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Specification** | 20% | Econometric model clearly specified |
| **Identification** | 30% | Causal identification approach rigorously justified |
| **Assumptions** | 20% | Key assumptions stated and defended, not merely asserted |
| **Robustness Depth** | 20% | Robustness approach is comprehensive and pre-specified |
| **Replicability** | 10% | Sufficient detail for replication |

**Section Importance Multiplier:** 1.3 *(Highest for empirical papers)*

**Scoring Anchors (NEW):**
- **Identification (5)**: Compelling argument with institutional detail; falsification tests planned; assumptions defended; comprehensive robustness plan pre-specified; replication-ready detail
- **Identification (3)**: Standard identification approach adequately explained; basic assumptions stated; typical robustness checks mentioned
- **Identification (1)**: Weak or circular identification reasoning; endogeneity unaddressed; assumptions hidden; robustness absent or ad hoc

- **Robustness Depth (5)**: Extensive pre-specified checks systematically exploring specifications, samples, and measures; addresses all major threats to inference
- **Robustness Depth (3)**: Standard robustness checks included (alternative specs, subsamples, variable definitions)
- **Robustness Depth (1)**: Only confirmatory tests shown; insufficient variation tested; cherry-picked checks

**Common Issues to Look For:**
- Vague or incomplete model specification
- Weak or absent identification strategy
- Unstated or unrealistic assumptions
- No robustness checks planned
- Insufficient methodological detail for replication

## Results
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Alignment** | 20% | Results directly address the stated research question |
| **Interpretation Depth** | 30% | Results interpreted with depth: economic magnitude quantified, mechanisms explained, compared to prior work |
| **Honesty** | 20% | Unexpected, null, or contradictory results acknowledged and explained |
| **Calibration** | 15% | Results calibrated to real-world context: effect sizes compared to benchmarks, policy implications quantified |
| **Presentation** | 15% | Tables and figures integrate well with narrative |

**Section Importance Multiplier:** 1.2

**Scoring Anchors (NEW):**
- **Interpretation Depth (5)**: Economic magnitudes quantified and calibrated to real-world benchmarks; mechanisms explained with potential decomposition; unexpected results addressed transparently; effect sizes compared to prior literature
- **Interpretation Depth (3)**: Basic discussion of economic magnitude beyond significance stars; main findings clearly presented; tables adequately described
- **Interpretation Depth (1)**: Only reports significance stars without interpretation; ignores null or contradictory results; no discussion of economic magnitude

- **Honesty (5)**: Transparent discussion with potential explanations for null/unexpected results
- **Honesty (3)**: Acknowledged briefly
- **Honesty (1)**: Ignored or cherry-picked; selective reporting

- **Calibration (5)**: Effect sizes compared to real-world benchmarks, policy costs/benefits quantified, magnitude placed in practical context
- **Calibration (3)**: Some mention of real-world magnitude
- **Calibration (1)**: No calibration to real-world context

**Common Issues to Look For:**
- Overemphasis on p-values without economic interpretation
- Cherry-picking significant results
- Tables/figures not properly integrated or explained
- Null or unexpected findings ignored or buried
- Results don't clearly answer the research question

## Proofs / Derivations
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Correctness** | 20% | No mathematical errors in derivations |
| **Rigor** | 25% | Technical conditions handled properly: convergence explicitly derived, transversality stated, edge cases addressed |
| **Economic Depth** | 25% | Economic mechanisms explained with depth beyond surface intuition: provides decomposition, welfare analysis, multi-layered interpretation |
| **Sophistication** | 15% | Analysis advances beyond routine techniques |
| **Clarity** | 15% | Derivation clear and accessible to expert readers |

**Section Importance Multiplier:** 1.4 *(Highest for theoretical papers)*

**Scoring Anchors (COMPLETELY REVISED):**

**Rigor:**
- **5 (Excellent)**: Convergence conditions explicitly derived (e.g., βᵞ E[e^(-γb + h)] < 1); transversality condition stated and interpreted; edge cases (e.g., h → ∞) discussed with economic interpretation; parameter restrictions justified
- **3 (Adequate)**: Mathematically correct with standard steps; basic derivation without deep technical treatment
- **1 (Poor)**: Logical gaps; hand-waving instead of rigor; missing convergence/transversality conditions; "after some algebra" without showing work

**Economic Depth:**
- **5 (Excellent)**: Multi-layered economic interpretation; decomposition analysis (e.g., consumption risk vs. disaster risk); welfare implications quantified; mechanisms explained beyond "X increases with Y"; connections to policy or empirical implications
- **3 (Adequate)**: One-sentence intuition per result; basic economic interpretation without depth
- **1 (Poor)**: Tautological interpretation ("X increases because investors want more X"); pure math without economic meaning

**Sophistication:**
- **5 (Excellent)**: Novel theoretical insight; creative proof technique; non-standard analysis
- **3 (Adequate)**: Competent standard analysis using well-established methods
- **1 (Poor)**: Trivial extensions; routine application

**MAJOR CHANGES:**
- **Correctness** reduced from 35% → 20% (correctness is necessary but not sufficient for quality)
- **NEW: Rigor** (25%) - Measures depth of technical treatment
- **NEW: Economic Depth** (25%) - Measures quality of economic interpretation
- **NEW: Sophistication** (15%) - Measures theoretical advancement
- **Clarity** reduced from 25% → 15%
- **Removed**: Logical Flow (25%), Completeness (20%), Intuition (20%) - integrated into new criteria

**Common Issues to Look For:**
- Mathematical errors or invalid steps
- Logical gaps in proof structure
- Missing intermediate steps
- Pure math without economic intuition
- Unjustified assumptions introduced mid-proof
- Missing convergence or transversality conditions
- Ad hoc parameter choices without empirical grounding

# SCORE CALCULATION EXAMPLE

## Example: Empirical Paper - Methodology Section

**Criteria Scores from LLM:**
- Specification: 4/5 (weight 20%)
- Identification: 5/5 (weight 30%)
- Assumptions: 3/5 (weight 20%)
- Robustness Depth: 4/5 (weight 20%)
- Replicability: 3/5 (weight 10%)

**Raw Section Score:**
```
= (4 × 0.20) + (5 × 0.30) + (3 × 0.20) + (4 × 0.20) + (3 × 0.10)
= 0.80 + 1.50 + 0.60 + 0.80 + 0.30
= 4.0 / 5.0
```

**Adjusted Section Score:**
```
= Raw Score × Importance Multiplier
= 4.0 × 1.3
= 5.2 / 5.0
(capped at 5.0)
```

**Impact:** The methodology section's strong performance (4.0) gets amplified to 5.2 (capped at 5.0) because methodology is crucial for empirical papers (multiplier 1.3).

# DISCRIMINATING EVALUATION PHILOSOPHY (NEW)

## Use the Full 1-5 Range

The system is now calibrated for **TOP ECONOMICS JOURNALS** (JPE, QJE, AER, JF, JFE, RFS).

### Scoring Guidelines

- **5 (Excellent)**: Publication-ready for top journals. Rigorous, insightful, novel. Pushes boundaries.
  - Example: Derives convergence conditions explicitly, provides decomposition analysis, calibrates to empirical estimates, discusses welfare implications

- **4 (Good)**: Strong work that needs refinement. Would be publishable in top field journals with revision.
  - Example: Rigorous derivation with some depth, but missing sophistication elements like decomposition or calibration

- **3 (Adequate)**: Meets MINIMUM standards but routine/shallow. Technically correct but no depth.
  - Example: Correct derivation with standard steps, one-sentence intuition, ad hoc parameters

- **2 (Below Average)**: Significant issues. Major revision needed. Not suitable for top journals.
  - Example: Derivation has gaps, intuition is superficial, identification weak

- **1 (Poor)**: Fundamental flaws. Not suitable for academic publication.
  - Example: Mathematical errors, circular reasoning, tautological statements

**CRITICAL**: Papers that merely "check boxes" (have assumptions, have results, mention intuition) should score **3 at most**. Avoid compression toward 3-4. If work is routine, score it 3. If insightful and rigorous, score it 4-5.

# SOPHISTICATION PRE-ASSESSMENT

Before scoring, evaluators must assess:

## For Theoretical/Model Sections:
1. **Mathematical rigor**: Convergence conditions derived? Transversality stated? Edge cases handled?
2. **Economic depth**: Intuition beyond "X increases with Y"? Mechanisms decomposed? Welfare discussed?
3. **Parameter realism**: Calibrated to data or ad hoc? Ranges economically justified?
4. **Completeness**: Full parameter space explored? Sensitivity analysis? Robustness checks?
5. **Literature integration**: Builds meaningfully on prior results, or just cites papers?

## For Empirical Sections:
1. **Identification rigor**: Convincingly argued with institutional detail, or merely asserted?
2. **Robustness comprehensiveness**: Extensive pre-specified, or minimal confirmatory?
3. **Economic interpretation**: Effect sizes calibrated to real-world? Or just significance stars?
4. **Honesty**: Null results and weakening cases discussed transparently?
5. **Data appropriateness**: Rigorously justified, or questionable with limitations dismissed?

**If most answers suggest basic/routine work**, scores should be 3 or below. Reserve 4-5 for sophisticated work.

# CRITERIA DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

## Universal Sections
- **Purpose:** Establish baseline quality expectations across all paper types
- **Focus:** Structure, clarity, intellectual contribution, academic rigor
- **Examples:** Abstract, Introduction, Literature Review, Conclusion

## Paper-Type Specific Sections
- **Purpose:** Evaluate specialized content unique to paper subgenres
- **Focus:** Technical rigor appropriate to methodology/approach
- **Examples:**
  - Empirical: Data quality, identification strategy, robustness
  - Theoretical: Mathematical rigor, proof correctness, model assumptions
  - Policy: Institutional accuracy, feasibility, stakeholder analysis

## Importance Multipliers
- **Purpose:** Reflect which sections matter most for each paper type
- **Logic:**
  - Empirical: Methodology and results are paramount
  - Theoretical: Proofs and model setup are critical
  - Policy: Recommendations and context are essential
- **Effect:** Strong performance in key sections has greater impact on overall score

## Scoring Scale (1-5)
- **1:** Major fundamental problems
- **2:** Significant weaknesses
- **3:** Adequate, meets basic standards *(BUT ROUTINE - NOT SUFFICIENT FOR TOP JOURNALS)*
- **4:** Good quality, above average
- **5:** Excellent, exemplary

# KEY IMPROVEMENTS SUMMARY (March 2024)

## New Criteria Added
- **Rigor** (Theoretical): Measures technical completeness
- **Economic Depth** (Theoretical): Measures quality of interpretation
- **Sophistication** (Theoretical/Empirical): Measures advancement beyond routine
- **Interpretation Depth** (Empirical): Multi-faceted results analysis

- **Calibration** (Empirical): Real-world context for effect sizes

## Concrete Scoring Anchors
All major criteria now have explicit 5/3/1 examples to anchor evaluator judgments and avoid score compression.

## Expected Impact
- **High-quality papers**: Score increase from ~3.5 to ~4.5
- **Low-quality papers**: Score decrease from ~3.0 to ~2.5
- **Discrimination**: Score spread increases from ~0.5 to ~2.0 points
